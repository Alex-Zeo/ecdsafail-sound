How it works · why this fork

The upstream grader seeds its 9024 test inputs from a Fiat-Shamir hash of your own circuit's op stream, so contestants re-roll which inputs are tested for free (the "tail-nonce hunt") and pass a circuit that is wrong on reachable inputs — that's why the headline sub-1300-qubit numbers are not correct circuits. This fork's sound_seed draws test inputs from a grader-controlled source independent of the ops, so nonce-hunting does nothing and only a genuinely-correct circuit passes. Will you beat Google? The target is their correct 1175-qubit ZKP-validated point-add — and it has to be beaten honestly.
